How's your district doing in science?

Recommendations from professional organizations and knowledgeable experts, along with a mountain of data (see here), indicate students are best prepared for education or training after high school if they took a full year each of Biology, Chemistry, and Physics. Florida requires Biology plus (nearly) any other two science courses to graduate high school. The plots below paint an unflattering picture of college and career readiness in our 67 school districts.

See something amiss? Drop a line at adamlamee at gmail dot com. If you'd like to be part of the solution, it's a worthy cause that could use your help.

About the data

Enrollment data comes form the wonderfully easy-to-access FL Dept of Education.

The plots were created by Adam LaMee. Charter and middle schools were not included. "1st-year" Biology, Chemistry, and Physics courses were identified as typical first exposure in the content area by the course codes for 1, 1 honors, pre-IB, pre-AP, and pre-AICE. For Physics, that also included AP 1, since some schools offer that as a first Physics course. Schools use unique progression plans and the creators of this page do not assume to know what those are on a site by site basis.
